Process: Glycolysis

Step 1: Analysis of Gene Functions

The genes HK1, GPI, PFKM, ALDOA, TPI1, GAPDH, PGK1, PGAM2, ENO3, and PKM are all involved in the process of glycolysis, a metabolic pathway that converts glucose into pyruvate.

- HK1 (Hexokinase 1) catalyzes the first step of glycolysis, converting glucose to glucose-6-phosphate.
- GPI (Glucose-6-phosphate isomerase) catalyzes the second step, converting glucose-6-phosphate into fructose 6-phosphate.
- PFKM (Phosphofructokinase, muscle) catalyzes the third step, converting fructose 6-phosphate to fructose 1,6-bisphosphate.
- ALDOA (Aldolase, fructose-bisphosphate A) catalyzes the fourth step, splitting fructose 1,6-bisphosphate into two three-carbon sugars.
- TPI1 (Triosephosphate isomerase) catalyzes the fifth step, converting dihydroxyacetone phosphate into glyceraldehyde 3-phosphate.
- GAPDH (Glyceraldehyde-3-phosphate dehydrogenase) catalyzes the sixth step, converting glyceraldehyde 3-phosphate into 1,3-bisphosphoglycerate.
- PGK1 (Phosphoglycerate kinase 1) catalyzes the seventh step, converting 1,3-bisphosphoglycerate into 3-phosphoglycerate.
- PGAM2 (Phosphoglycerate mutase 2) catalyzes the eighth step, converting 3-phosphoglycerate into 2-phosphoglycerate.
- ENO3 (Enolase 3) catalyzes the ninth step, converting 2-phosphoglycerate into phosphoenolpyruvate.
- PKM (Pyruvate kinase, muscle) catalyzes the final step, converting phosphoenolpyruvate into pyruvate.

Step 2: Critical Analysis of Gene Functional Associations

The genes in this set are functionally associated as they all participate in the glycolysis pathway. This pathway is a series of enzymatic reactions that degrade glucose to produce pyruvate, which is further metabolized to generate ATP, the cell's main source of energy. Each gene encodes an enzyme that catalyzes a specific step in this pathway, indicating a high degree of functional interdependence.
